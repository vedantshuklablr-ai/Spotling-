ğŸ“± Social Media Safety

ğŸ”’ Protect Your Social Media Account

Privacy Settings

  • Set profile to "Private" or "Friends Only"
  • Limit who can see your posts and information
  • Disable location tagging on photos
  • Turn off search engine indexing of your profile

What NOT to Post

  • Real-time location or plans (share after, not during)
  • Personal documents, passport photos, IDs
  • Family member's names or relationships
  • Daily routines or when you'll be away

Scam Prevention

  • Don't click suspicious links in messages
  • Be suspicious of people asking personal questions
  • Don't send money to online "friends" you haven't met
  • Verify friend requests from people you know (account could be fake)

Bot & Fake Account Detection

  • No profile picture or generic images = suspicious
  • Brand new account with few followers = fake
  • Unusual follower count vs. engagement rate
  • Spam or repetitive comments = bot

ğŸ›’ Online Shopping Safety

Before You Buy

  • Research seller: Check reviews on independent sites
  • Verify website: Visit through official website, not links from ads/emails
  • Check security: Look for padlock icon and "https://" in URL
  • Compare prices: Extremely low prices often indicate fakes
  • Read return policy: Ensure 30+ days return/refund available

Payment Methods

  • Best: Credit card (fraud protection), digital wallets (Google Pay, Apple Pay)
  • Good: Debit card with fraud protection enabled
  • Risky: Cash on delivery (if seller is unknown), Bank transfer
  • Avoid: Cryptocurrency, Direct bank transfer, Gift cards

Red Flags

  • Sellers only accepting cash/cryptocurrency
  • Asking you to buy from different website
  • No way to contact seller customer service
  • Images appear AI-generated or heavily edited
  • Unrealistic discounts (80% off on new electronics)

ğŸ†˜ If Something Goes Wrong

If You Think You've Been Hacked

  1. STOP using the device immediately for sensitive activities
  2. Change passwords from a different safe computer
  3. Enable 2FA if not already active
  4. Check account activity for unauthorized changes
  5. Scan device with antivirus software
  6. Run malware removal tools
  7. Contact affected services (email, bank, social media)
  8. If needed, consider professional cybersecurity help

If You've Shared Sensitive Information

  1. Change the password immediately
  2. Enable 2FA on that account
  3. Monitor the account closely
  4. Check if it's used for password recovery on other accounts
  5. If it was a financial account, contact the institution
  6. Freeze credit if it was financial data
  7. File a fraud report if money was lost

If You've Transferred Money by Mistake

  1. Contact your bank IMMEDIATELY (don't wait)
  2. Provide transaction details and recipient information
  3. Ask bank to initiate reversal (within 24 hours possible)
  4. File cyber crime complaint (cybercrime.gov.in)
  5. Get FIR number from police
  6. Document everything for legal proceedings
