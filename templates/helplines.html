âš ï¸ ALERT: Fake Police Calls & Cyber Fraud

ğŸš¨ Fake Police Call Scam - WARNING

What it is: Scammers impersonate police officers or officials claiming you have a criminal case, illegal money, or bank fraud. They demand payment or personal details.

Common Tactics:

  • Call from fake "+91" numbers or +1 (international showing)
  • Claim there's an FIR against you
  • "Your Aadhaar is being used for illegal activities"
  • "Money laundering case registered in your name"
  • Threaten arrest if you don't pay or share details
  • Ask you to transfer money to "safe account"
  • Demand you not tell anyone or you'll be arrested
  • Use official police station names/codes to sound authentic

âš ï¸ RED FLAGS - THIS IS ALWAYS A SCAM:

  • Legitimate police NEVER calls to collect money
  • Police NEVER threaten on phone and demand payments
  • Police NEVER ask for UPI/bank transfer
  • Police NEVER ask you to keep it secret
  • Police NEVER ask for Aadhaar/PAN via phone
  • If you're being arrested, police come in person with warrant

What to do if you get such a call:

  1. HANG UP IMMEDIATELY - Do not engage with the scammer
  2. Do NOT ask questions or argue - This keeps you on the line
  3. Do NOT provide any personal information
  4. Do NOT transfer any money under any circumstances
  5. Report to Police: File FIR at local police station with the caller's number
  6. Block the number: Mark as spam/fraud on your phone
  7. Report to Cyber Cell: Visit cybercrime.gov.in and report the number
  8. Verify directly: Call the actual police station on their official number to verify any claims

ğŸ™ï¸ Fake Security/Bank Trust Officer Calls

Common scenarios:

  • "Your bank account is being used for fraud"
  • "We detected suspicious activity on your account"
  • "Your payment got declined, verify your card"
  • "You have unclaimed insurance/tax refund"

âš ï¸ Remember: Banks NEVER call to ask for OTP, password, or card details. If you receive such a call, hang up and call the bank's official number yourself.

ğŸ–¥ï¸ Fake Cyber Cell / IT Act Threats

Scam Pattern:

  • Fake caller claims "Cyber Cell" is tracking you
  • Claims you downloaded illegal content
  • Threatens prosecution under IT Act
  • Demands payment to settle the case

Truth: Real Cyber Cell investigations happen officially via police stations, NOT via random calls. They don't demand money to settle cases.
