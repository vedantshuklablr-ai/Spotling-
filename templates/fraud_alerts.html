üéØ Common Fraud and Scam Types

‚ùå Phishing Scams

What it is: Fraudsters impersonate legitimate companies to steal your personal information, passwords, or banking details through fake emails, SMS, or links.

Red Flags:

  • Requests for password or OTP via email/SMS
  • Urgent action required messages
  • Suspicious links from "official" companies
  • Grammar errors in official communications

How to Protect: Never click links in unsolicited emails, verify URLs before clicking, enable 2FA, and call the company directly to verify if unsure.

üí≥ Online Payment Fraud

What it is: Scammers conduct fraudulent transactions, duplicate cards, or use stolen payment information for unauthorized purchases.

Red Flags:

  • Unexpected payment requests from known vendors
  • Suspicious transaction notifications
  • Being asked to share card/UPI details
  • Requests to pay in advance with untraceable methods

How to Protect: Use secure payment gateways, never share OTP/CVV, monitor bank statements regularly, and report unauthorized transactions immediately.

üéÅ Prize/Lottery Scams

What it is: You receive notifications that you've won a prize or lottery you never entered, and are asked to pay fees to claim it.

Red Flags:

  • Winning claims from unknown lotteries
  • Requests for upfront payment to claim prize
  • Pressure to act immediately
  • Low quality graphics or poor language

How to Protect: Never pay money to claim prizes, verify the lottery officially, and remember legitimate lotteries don't ask for advance fees.

üë§ Identity Theft

What it is: Criminals steal your personal information and use it to open accounts, make purchases, or apply for loans in your name.

Red Flags:

  • Unexpected bills or account notifications
  • Loan rejections due to accounts you didn't open
  • Missing mail or documents
  • Requests for Aadhaar/PAN numbers from unknown sources

How to Protect: Safeguard your Aadhaar/PAN, don't share personal documents casually, monitor credit reports, and freeze credit if compromised.

üíº Job/Employment Scams

What it is: Fake job offers that require upfront payment for training, placement fees, or processing charges.

Red Flags:

  • Job offers via unsolicited messages
  • No proper interview process
  • Requests for money upfront
  • Too-good-to-be-true salary offers

How to Protect: Apply through official company websites, verify company contact details independently, never pay upfront, and ask for details in writing.

üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß Romance/Catfishing Scams

What it is: Scammers create fake profiles to build emotional relationships and then ask for money under false pretenses.

Red Flags:

  • Refusal to video chat despite long conversations
  • Quick profession of love and emotional dependence
  • Requests for money for emergencies
  • Stolen or professional photos as profile pictures

How to Protect: Video verify before meeting, don't send money to people you haven't met, be skeptical of sudden love declarations, and report suspicious profiles.

üè† Real Estate Scams

What it is: Unauthorized listings, fake rental agreements, or asking for advance payments for properties that don't belong to the scammer.

Red Flags:

  • Properties listed below market rate
  • Unwillingness to provide legal documentation
  • Requests for cash payments only
  • Pressure to decide quickly

How to Protect: Verify property documents, deal with registered agents, don't make payments before document verification, and involve a lawyer.

üì± Tech Support Scams

What it is: Pop-ups or calls claiming your device has viruses, asking you to call a number and give remote access or money.

Red Flags:

  • Unsolicited pop-ups claiming security issues
  • Unknown callers claiming to be from tech companies
  • Pressure to give remote access
  • Requests for payment in gift cards or cryptocurrency

How to Protect: Don't click unknown pop-ups, verify caller identity independently, run antivirus from trusted sources, and never give remote access to strangers.

üéì Educational Scams

What it is: Fake scholarships, admissions to foreign universities, or certification programs that require upfront payment.

Red Flags:

  • Guaranteed admissions without proper applications
  • Unconditional scholarship offers
  • Requests for processing or registration fees upfront
  • Lack of proper institutional credentials

How to Protect: Contact institutions directly, check accreditation officially, verify through government databases, and be wary of guaranteed offers.

üõ°Ô∏è General Safety Guidelines

‚úÖ What You Should Do

  • Verify identities through official channels
  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Keep software and antivirus updated
  • Read terms and policies before signing
  • Save receipts and documentation
  • Monitor bank statements regularly
  • Report suspicious activities immediately

‚ùå What You Should NOT Do

  • Share passwords, OTP, or CVV
  • Click links from unknown sources
  • Give remote access to strangers
  • Send money before verification
  • Download from untrusted sources
  • Share Aadhaar/PAN carelessly
  • Respond to unsolicited calls/messages
  • Ignore security warnings on websites

üÜò If You Fall Victim to Fraud

  1. Stop Losing Money: Immediately change passwords, block accounts, and prevent further unauthorized access.
  2. Report to Police: File FIR at local police station or cybercrime cell. Get the reference number.
  3. Notify Banks/Services: Contact your bank, email provider, and other relevant services immediately.
  4. Monitor Accounts: Check credit reports, watch for unauthorized accounts opened in your name.
  5. Document Everything: Keep records of all fraudulent transactions, communications, and police complaints.
  6. Seek Legal Help: Consult a lawyer for guidance on next steps and potential recovery.
  7. Contact Helplines: Reach out to fraud helplines and consumer protection organizations (see Helplines page).
