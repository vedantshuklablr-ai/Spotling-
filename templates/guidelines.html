üì± Personal Data Protection Bill (Data Protection Laws)

üí∞ RBI Guidelines (Digital Payment & Banking Fraud)

  • Fraud Reporting: Report to bank immediately; bank must investigate within 10 days

üìù How to File a Consumer Complaint

Step 1: Gather Documentation

Collect receipts, invoices, product photos, payment proof, and communication records with the seller/company.

Step 2: Try Amicable Resolution

First, try to resolve with the company directly through email, letter, or consumer grievance portal.

Step 3: File Complaint Online

Visit CCPA portal (www.cc.gov.in) or your state consumer commission website to file online complaint.

Step 4: Pay Prescribed Fee

Pay filing fee based on claim amount (‚Çπ100-‚Çπ5,000 typically for district commission).

Step 5: Wait for Hearing & Decision

Commission will schedule hearing, listen to both parties, and issue decision within 90 days.

Step 6: Enforcement & Appeal

If unfavorable, you can appeal to State Commission or National Commission.

‚öñÔ∏è Penalties & Punishments for Fraudsters

Criminal Penalties Include:

  • Imprisonment (ranging from 6 months to life)
  • Fines (ranging from ‚Çπ1,000 to ‚Çπ10+ lakh)
  • Seizure of proceeds of crime
  • Asset forfeiture

Civil Penalties Include:

  • Compensation to victims
  • Damages (up to 3x the actual loss)
  • Business license suspension/cancellation
  • Embargo on future business operations

Additional Consequences:

  • Criminal record that affects employment
  • Bank account freezing and asset seizure
  • Potential travel bans
  • Registration as a habitual offender
